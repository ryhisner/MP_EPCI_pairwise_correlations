All non-RBD mutations that occurred at least twice as private mutations in EPCI sequences were tested against all other mutations to see how often they occurred together and whether they occurred together more often than expected by chance. If the matched mutations occur in one of the name mutation patterns from the paper, that is listed in the right-hand column. There are also a few additional categories included, such as "BA.3.2-like," since a significant number of EPCI sequences carry mutation patterns that bear a striking resemblence to BA.3.2.
